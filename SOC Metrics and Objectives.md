## Overview

A Security Operations Center (SOC) isn't just about detecting and responding to cyber attacks. To understand whether a SOC is performing well, we need measurable objectives and performance metrics.

This room introduced the key metrics used by SOC teams to evaluate their effectiveness, identify bottlenecks, and continuously improve incident response.

---

## What I Learned

### Why SOC Metrics Matter

SOC metrics help security teams answer questions like:

* Are we detecting threats quickly?
* Are analysts responding within the expected time?
* Are we closing incidents efficiently?
* Is the SOC improving over time?

Without proper metrics, it's difficult to know whether the team is actually becoming more effective.

---

### Important Metrics

#### Mean Time to Detect (MTTD)

Measures how long it takes to detect a security incident after it begins.

**Lower MTTD = Faster detection**

---

#### Mean Time to Acknowledge (MTTA)

Measures the time between an alert being generated and an analyst beginning the investigation.

A lower MTTA means alerts are being picked up quickly.

---

#### Mean Time to Respond (MTTR)

Measures how long it takes to contain and remediate an incident after detection.

Reducing MTTR minimizes the impact of security incidents.

---

#### Service Level Agreements (SLAs)

SLAs define the expected response times for different alert severities.

For example:

* Critical alerts → Respond within 15 minutes
* High alerts → Respond within 1 hour
* Medium alerts → Respond within 4 hours

These targets help SOC teams stay consistent and prioritize work effectively.

---

#### False Positives

False positives are alerts that appear malicious but are actually harmless.

A high false positive rate can:

* Waste analyst time
* Increase alert fatigue
* Delay investigations of real threats

Reducing unnecessary alerts allows analysts to focus on genuine security incidents.

---

## Key Takeaways

* SOC performance should be measured, not assumed.
* Metrics help identify strengths and weaknesses in incident response.
* Faster detection and response reduce the impact of attacks.
* SLAs keep incident handling consistent across the team.
* Lowering false positives improves both efficiency and analyst productivity.
* Metrics are meant to improve processes, not simply measure speed.
